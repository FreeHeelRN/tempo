# Tempo — Vision & Framework

## The Problem
Apps like Yuka give food a universal score that means nothing to the individual. A protein bar gets penalized the same way for a bodybuilder and a diabetic. That's lazy and misleading.

## The Solution
Tempo scores food against YOUR goals, not some arbitrary universal standard. The same Greek yogurt means something different to someone trying to lose weight vs. someone managing blood sugar vs. someone optimizing for longevity. Tempo shows you that context.

## The Experts Behind It
Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, Sachin Panda, Rob Lustig, and Tim Spector. Their frameworks are woven into every food analysis — naturally, not clinically. It should feel like getting advice from a friend who actually knows this stuff.

## Key Truths
- **The scale lies.** Muscle gain masks fat loss. Body composition (DEXA, Hume Pod scale) tells the real story.
- **Sleep is nutrition.** Poor sleep raises cortisol and ghrelin and undoes good eating.
- **When you eat matters** as much as what you eat (Panda/circadian).
- **Sugar and ultra-processed food** aren't just "unhealthy" — Lustig has the receipts on exactly why.
- **Your microbiome is personal.** The same food affects different people differently (Spector).
- **The single most consistent healthy habit:** walk daily with someone you love, phones off.

## Tone
Encouraging, not preachy. Celebrate good choices. Redirect bad ones gently. Sound like a knowledgeable friend, not a lecture.

## Scoring Framework
1–10 Nutrition Quality Scale:
- **1:** Candy, soda (actively harmful)
- **8:** Good whole foods
- **9-10:** Precision nutrition — same food, but ingredient quality and portion intentionality matter

The differentiator between 8, 9, and 10 isn't just what the food is — it's how.

## User Flow
1. Set goals (body composition, health conditions, dietary preferences)
2. **Set sleep quality** (key input — affects analysis)
3. Scan/search food
4. Get personalized analysis based on YOUR context
5. Community-powered database — if you scan something missing, you add it for the next person

## Analysis Style
NOT: "This has 12g of sugar (medium risk)"  
YES: "12g added sugar — Lustig's research shows this spikes insulin and promotes fat storage, especially problematic for your goal of fat loss. If you're eating this post-workout, timing helps, but otherwise, look for <5g sugar options."

Weave in expert frameworks naturally. Reference sleep if it's poor. Note circadian timing if relevant. Mention microbiome when fiber/fermented foods come up.
